# Bold – Unmoderated User Research Script
**Platform:** UserBrain
**Study type:** Unmoderated remote usability test (think-aloud)
**Estimated time:** 15–20 minutes
**Prototype link:** [insert link]

---

## Screener

**Question:** How would you describe your current exercise habits?

- [ ] I rarely or never exercise ✓
- [ ] I used to exercise but have stopped ✓
- [ ] I do light activity occasionally (walks, stretching) ✓
- [ ] I exercise regularly, 3 or more times per week ✗ *Disqualify*

---

## Welcome Message
*(Shown to participant before the study begins)*

> Thank you for participating! We're testing a new service called Bold. We want to understand your honest first impressions — there are no right or wrong answers.
>
> As you go through the experience, please talk out loud and share what you're thinking, feeling, or noticing. We'll give you a scenario to read before you start, and ask you a few questions along the way.
>
> You cannot break anything. Just explore naturally.

---

## Scenario
*(Read this before you begin)*

> Imagine you recently saw an ad that said Bold is a free exercise program covered by your Medicare Advantage plan. You were curious and decided to check it out. You clicked the ad, landed on their website, and decided to sign up.
>
> Go ahead and do that now. Think out loud as you go.

**Start URL:** `check-eligibility.html`

---

## Task 1 — Sign Up
*(Participant completes onboarding — no questions asked)*

> Complete the sign-up process. When you're done, you'll land on a new page — stop there and wait for the next question.

---

## Task 1 Questions — Your Plan Page

> You've just finished signing up. Look at the page in front of you.

**Q1 — Open text**
In your own words, what is this page telling you?

**Q2 — Open text**
Does the plan described here feel like it was made specifically for you, or does it feel like something everyone gets? Tell us why.

**Q3 — Rating scale (1–5)**
How confident do you feel that Bold understands what you need?
*1 = Not confident at all → 5 = Very confident*

**Q4 — Open text**
Knowing this is supposed to be covered by your Medicare Advantage plan at no cost — does this page make that feel worth it? What makes you say that?

**Q5 — Open text**
Is there anything missing from this page that you'd want to see before moving forward?

---

## Task 2 — Today's Plan
*(Participant taps "See today's plan")*

> Tap the button that says "See today's plan." When you get to the next page, take a moment to look around before doing anything. Talk out loud about what you see.

---

## Task 2 Questions — Today's Plan Page

**Q6 — Open text**
Without scrolling — what do you think this page is for? Describe it in your own words.

**Q7 — Open text**
There's a person shown at the top of the page. Who do you think they are, and what is their role?

**Q8 — Multiple choice**
How does the trainer make you feel about starting the class?
- [ ] More motivated — I like having a real person guiding me
- [ ] It doesn't change how I feel either way
- [ ] Less motivated — I'd prefer just the class information
- [ ] I'm not sure

**Q9 — Open text**
Is there anything on this page that confused you or that you didn't understand?

**Q10 — Rating scale (1–5)**
How motivated do you feel to start this class right now?
*1 = Not motivated at all → 5 = Very motivated*

**Q11 — Open text**
What would you do next on this page?

---

## Task 3 — Plan Summary
*(Participant taps "Plan summary")*

> Find the "Plan summary" button and tap it. Take a look at this page and talk out loud about what you see.

---

## Task 3 Questions — Plan Summary Page

**Q12 — Open text**
What do you think this page is showing you? Describe it in your own words.

**Q13 — Open text**
Look at the section called "Your journey." What does it tell you about where you are and where you're headed?

**Q14 — Open text**
The first step says "You made the decision." What do you think of that?

**Q15 — Open text**
Does this page feel connected to the questions Bold asked you during sign-up, or does it feel unrelated?

**Q16 — Rating scale (1–5)**
How confident do you feel that Bold has built the right plan for you?
*1 = Not confident at all → 5 = Very confident*

**Q17 — Open text**
Is there anything on this page you'd want to see that's missing?

---

## Closing Questions

**Q18 — Open text**
Think about the whole experience from start to finish. What stood out most — good or bad?

**Q19 — Open text**
Was there any moment where you almost wanted to stop? What happened?

**Q20 — Open text**
Who do you think Bold is designed for? Is it for someone like you?

**Q21 — Multiple choice**
Would you use Bold if it was free through your Medicare Advantage plan?
- [ ] Yes, definitely
- [ ] Yes, probably
- [ ] Not sure
- [ ] Probably not
- [ ] Definitely not

**Q22 — Open text**
What's one thing Bold could do to make you more likely to use it?

## Research Team Notes
*(Not shown to participants)*

### What to watch for in recordings

| Area | Signal |
|---|---|
| Medicare framing | Does the free/covered framing increase trust, or raise skepticism? |
| Results page | Do they read the bullets? Feel it's personal or boilerplate? |
| Trainer | Do they notice/name the trainer unprompted? Do they read the intro text? |
| Trainer value | "Feels personal / reassuring" vs "doesn't matter to me" |
| Today's plan clarity | Do they immediately understand it's a workout page? Find the Start button? |
| Plan summary | Do they connect it to their sign-up answers? Read the journey section? |
| Emotional language | Listen for: confusing, overwhelming, not for me, feels real, feels generic |

### Hypotheses to validate

- **H1:** The results page feels personalized, not generic
- **H2:** The Medicare Advantage framing increases trust and perceived value
- **H3:** Users understand the trainer is a coach, not a stock photo
- **H4:** The trainer increases motivation to start the class
- **H5:** The plan summary feels emotionally resonant and connected to their sign-up
- **H6:** "You made the decision" lands as motivating, not patronizing
- **H7:** Users can navigate from today's plan to plan summary without confusion
